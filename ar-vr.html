EECS 440 P3 - Camping Simulator

A simulated campground for individual or teaching use. The user experiences a wide variety of camping activities and learns through both experience and instruction what to do (or what not to do) in order to stay safe and be more environmentally conscious."

Project Background

Experience the complete VR camping adventure: cast your line to master fishing, gather sticks and spark campfires, hang your food high to keep bears away, spot poison ivy before it spots you, and learn simple steps to prevent wildfires. Each activity teaches real-world safety skills so you can explore—and enjoy—the great outdoors with confidence.

  • Learn More

Technologies Used

The simulation was built using Unreal Engine 5, leveraging high-fidelity rendering, physics-based interactions, and VR support to create an immersive experience.

  • Game Engine: Unreal Engine 5
  • VR Support: Meta Quest Pro
  • 3D Assets: Sketchfab, Blender
  • Interactive Elements: Blueprints

EECS 440 P1 - CSE Simulator

VR Simulation/Interactive Digital Twin of BBB 1695 at the University of Michigan

Project Background

This project aims to recreate the 1695 BBB (Beyster Building) lab at the University of Michigan as an interactive VR environment. It serves as an educational and exploratory tool, allowing users to experience the space, interact with objects, and understand the history of the location.
